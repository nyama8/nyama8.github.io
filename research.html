Research

Research interests

My research interests are primarily in the intersection of physics and engineering focusing on the development and implementation of technologies for quantum information processing.

Current and past projects

Liquid-Metal (LM) reconfigurable electronics

PI: Aaron Ohta and Wayne Shiroma, Milimeter wave electronics lab, 2018-2020

Room-temperature liquid metals (LM) can be used to enable reconfigurability in radio-frequency (RF) electronics. The devices can be reconfigured using voltage signals by means of continuous electrowetting, which requires an immersing electrolyte solution. My work on this project was primarily concerned with characterizing the effect of the electrolyte [J.1] and modeling their effect in these devices [C.4].

Valley physics in 2D materials coupled to photonic structures

PI: Vinod Menon, Laboratory for Nano and Micro Photonics, 2018-2020

Transition metal dichalcogenides (TMDCs) are a class of layered Van er Waals materials that can be reduced to layers only a few atoms thick. In this limit, TMDCs exhibit a variety of unique optical and electronic properties including the valley degree-of-freedom, which could potentialy serve as qubit. My work on this project was primarily focused on characterizing the valley properties when coupled to photonic structures [C.1-3].
